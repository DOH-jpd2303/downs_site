Introduction

Generic Organization (GO) offers mental health (MH) benefits to eligible clients. Each benefit is placed into a level of care. This tier determines both the amount of services expected from providers and the total payment that will be delivered. Currently, providers request a tier level for their clients at the beginning of the benefit. Typically, a client maintains the same tier for the entire length of the authorization.

In 2019, work began to change the way clients were assigned to levels of care. Instead of providers requesting tiers of service at the start of an authorization, tiers will be assigned quarterly based on a scoring system maintained by GO. This scoring system is called the Generic Health Stratification (GHS) model.

Payment will also change in the new model. Each quarter, clients will be evaluated for hours of care received versus hours of care expected based on their tier level. This comparison of expected versus actual services delivered is called Service Delivery Adherence (SDA). Future payments will be adjusted based on a client’s recent SDA performance at a provider agency.

This document describes the methodology for GHS model and SDA payment adjustment. This document will also describe what to expect during the transition period, including what resources will be available to providers. Descriptions of the GHS scoring model, Service Delivery Adherence (SDA) process and other changes will be described in detail. Appendices are also provided, including a technical reference for how model inputs were determined; examples of SDA calculations; a description of fields in the preliminary files distributed to providers; and a summary of changes to the GHS model, process, and files delivered to providers.

GHS Scoring and Level of Care Assignment

Determining a client’s GHS score

Each authorization will be assigned a GHS score quarterly. Continuing benefits will receive GHS scores on the first day of each quarter. New benefits will receive GHS scores within a week of receiving all necessary information from the requesting party.

There are two different scoring systems based on the age of the client:

Assigning level of care from GHS score

Client level of care will be assigned based on the quarterly GHS score. See Table 1 and Table 2 for assignments for adult and child benefits, respectively. This level of care will indicate both the monthly base payment amount and the expected monthly hours a client is to receive. This level of care assignment will be used for the entire quarter.

Service Delivery Adherence (SDA)

Overview

A benefit’s expected level of care and payment amount will be based on their PHS model score and level of care assignment for the quarter. Both are summarized in Table 4. All monthly payments will be prorated by the number of days the benefit was active during the month. For ease of interpretability, payment amounts in Table 4 are shown per 30 days of active benefit. This was done to estimate a ‘monthly’ payment.

In addition to using the PHS scoring model to determine a client’s tier each quarter, monthly payment amounts will be adjusted based on an agency’s performance in meeting prior service delivery targets for the client. This adjustment is called service delivery adherence (SDA), and will be described in more detail below.

Calculation of SDA

Active benefits will receive a service delivery adherence (SDA) calculation once each quarter, if possible. SDA percentages are the ratio of the amount of services a client received relative to the amount requested for all clients at a certain benefit level during a period. Or:

Examples of SDA calculations, including expected hours, prorated monthly service hours, and adjustments to payment are provided in Appendix B. SDA will not be calculated unless the client had 14 or more days of active benefit during the SDA calculation period. A suspended benefit is considered inactive. If SDA cannot be calculated for the most recent quarter, results from a prior SDA calculation will be used. Rules for using prior quarter SDA statistics are highlighted below:

  • New authorizations (no prior stratification score)
    1. If an agency served the same client within 90 days of the new benefit beginning, the most recent SDA statistic from this benefit will be used.
    2. If the agency has not served the same client with the same type of benefit (MH or SUD) within the 90 days, SDA is not available for the client.
  • Authorizations which have been previously stratified
    1. If SDA can be calculated for the quarter, payment adjustment will be based on that SDA.
    2. If SDA cannot be calculated for the quarter, the most recently available SDA calculation will be used for the client. Results from a prior authorization may be used if an agency served the same client under the same type of benefit (MH or SUD) within 90 days of the current benefit start date.
