The Tiger King

Joe Exotic, respector of no persons

An amazing confluence of circumstances brought us here today. As I write, the coronavirus is sweeping through the country, bringing life to a stand-still. As I work from home and eat through the canned goods and bread we bought in a panic, learning HTML has become a new pastime. After doing some initial lessons, I learned about my first project: to create a tribute page.

Typically, I struggle with identified the men and women I aspire to be like. Those who deserve our respect as individuals, as a country. That was not the case this time. I knew immediately who I would give this honor.

Joe Exotic.

Joseph Moldonado-Passage is a man living near Wynnewood, Oklahoma. Coincidentally, this is less than a three-hour drive from my own hometown. Joe's Bio speaks of a wild and vivid history. Joe continuously skirted law enforcement, at least for a time.

Alright, I cannot continue to pretend that Joe is a role model. He has been convicted of murder-for-hire and various animal welfare charges . But, I refuse to turn this simple project into a lecture about the dangers of minimizing such atrocious behavior in the name of entertainment. We need this right now. We need something trashy, something off the wall. Something that draws us in while making no sense whatsoever. We have chosen Joe Exotic as the first true meme of the crisis. So be it.

So enjoy your 15 minutes, Joe Exotic. But let's stay in jail and think about what we did for a little longer, okay?
